  • Harajuku is a fascinating subculture movement encompassing a range of different looks, styles and communities, typically found in fashionable districts of Tokyo.
  • Harajuku style refers to the Harajuku district, a large, urban fashion neighbourhood in Tokyo.
  • Harajuku doesn't describe one particular style or way of looking, but the conglomeration of many different styles in one place.
  • The subculture of Harajuku fashion styles focuses primarily on two concerns – community and freedom of expression.
  • There is some debate about what Harajuku style represents. Some believe it's a rebellion against Japan's relatively strict societal rules and norms.
  • Alternatively, some view Harajuku fashion styles as just a celebration of personal freedom of expression.
